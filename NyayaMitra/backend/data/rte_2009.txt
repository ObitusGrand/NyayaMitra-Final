THE RIGHT OF CHILDREN TO FREE AND COMPULSORY EDUCATION ACT, 2009

Section 3. Right of children to free and compulsory education.
(1) Every child of the age of six to fourteen years shall have a right to free and compulsory education in a neighbourhood school till completion of elementary education.
(2) No child shall be liable to pay any kind of fee or charges or expenses which may prevent him from pursuing and completing the elementary education.

Section 12. Extent of school's responsibility for free and compulsory education.
(1)(c) Unaided private schools shall admit in class I, to the extent of at least twenty-five per cent of the strength of that class, children belonging to weaker section and disadvantaged group.

Section 13. No school or person shall subject the child to any admission test.
(1) No child shall be denied admission in a school for lack of age proof.

Section 16. No child admitted in a school shall be held back in any class or expelled from school till the completion of elementary education.

Section 17. Prohibition of physical punishment and mental harassment.
No child shall be subjected to physical punishment or mental harassment. Whoever contravenes this shall be liable to disciplinary action under the service rules.

Section 18. No school shall be established without obtaining a certificate of recognition.
