THE MATERNITY BENEFIT ACT, 1961
(Act No. 53 of 1961)

Section 5. Right to payment of maternity benefit.
(1) Every woman shall be entitled to, and her employer shall be liable for, the payment of maternity benefit at the rate of the average daily wage for the period of her actual absence immediately preceding and including the day of her delivery and for the six weeks immediately following that day.
(3) The maximum period for which any woman shall be entitled to maternity benefit shall be twenty-six weeks, of which not more than eight weeks shall precede the expected date of her delivery.

Section 5A. Continuity of maternity benefit on death of a woman.
If a woman entitled to maternity benefit dies before receiving such benefit, the employer shall pay the maternity benefit to the nominee or legal representative.

Section 11. Dismissal during absence.
When a woman absents herself from work in accordance with the provisions of this Act, it shall be unlawful for her employer to discharge or dismiss her during or on account of such absence. Any discharge or dismissal shall be void and of no effect.

Section 12. No deduction of wages in certain cases.
No deduction from the normal and usual daily wages of a woman shall be made by reason only of the nature of work assigned to her during pregnancy on account of a request made under section 4.
