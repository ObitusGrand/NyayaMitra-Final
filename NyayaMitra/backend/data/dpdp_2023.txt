DIGITAL PERSONAL DATA PROTECTION ACT, 2023
(Act No. 22 of 2023)

Section 4. Consent and legitimate uses.
A person may process the personal data of a Data Principal only in accordance with the provisions of this Act and for a lawful purpose— for which the Data Principal has given her consent, or for certain legitimate uses.

Section 5. Notice.
Every Data Fiduciary shall, before seeking consent, give to the Data Principal an intimation in clear and plain language containing a description of personal data sought and the purpose for which it is being processed.

Section 6. Consent.
Consent given by the Data Principal shall be free, specific, informed, unconditional and unambiguous with a clear affirmative action. The Data Principal shall have the right to withdraw her consent at any time.

Section 8. Rights of Data Principal.
(1) Right to access information about personal data.
(3) Right to correction and erasure of personal data.
(5) Right to nominate any other individual who shall exercise the rights of the Data Principal in the event of death or incapacity.
(7) Right of grievance redressal.

Section 9. Obligations of Data Fiduciary.
(1) A Data Fiduciary shall make reasonable efforts to ensure the accuracy and completeness of personal data.
(4) A Data Fiduciary shall protect personal data in its possession or under its control by taking reasonable security safeguards to prevent personal data breach.
(6) A Data Fiduciary shall, upon withdrawal of consent by the Data Principal, erase the personal data.

Section 15. Penalty.
The Board may impose monetary penalty up to two hundred and fifty crore rupees for significant data breaches arising from failure to implement reasonable security safeguards.
