THE EMPLOYEES' PROVIDENT FUNDS AND MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS ACT, 1952

Section 2. Definitions.
(f) "employer" means the owner or occupier of the factory and includes the managing agent, the legal representative of a deceased employer and, where a person has been named as a manager of the factory, the person so named.

Section 6. Contributions and matters which may be provided for in Schemes.
The contribution which shall be paid by the employer to the Fund shall be twelve per cent of the basic wages, dearness allowance and retaining allowance, if any, for the time being payable to each employee. The employee shall also contribute an equal amount.

Section 7A. Determination of moneys due from employers.
The Central Provident Fund Commissioner may determine the amount due from any employer, and for this purpose may conduct such inquiry as he deems necessary.

Section 7Q. Interest payable by the employer.
The employer shall be liable to pay simple interest at the rate of twelve per cent per annum or at such higher rate as may be specified on any amount due from him under this Act from the date on which the amount has become so due.

Section 14. Penalties.
(1) Whoever makes default in the payment of any contribution or any charges payable to the Employees' Provident Fund shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years but which shall not be less than one year and shall also be liable to fine of ten thousand rupees. In case of repeated default, imprisonment up to five years.
(2) Any employer who deducts the employee's contribution from wages but fails to deposit the same with the Fund shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term not less than one year extending to three years and fine.
